Acceptable Use Policy

Scope: This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties accessing the firm's IT systems, including hardware, software, network services, email accounts, and cloud resources. It governs both company-provided and personal devices used for business operations.

Acceptable Use

  • Use company systems and resources only for authorized business activities.
  • Protect login credentials and access rights; use strong passwords and enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) where applicable.
  • Ensure compliance with company security policies, data protection laws, and industry standards.
  • Maintain the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of company data.
  • Use company-provided software and applications in accordance with licensing agreements.

Prohibited Activities

  • Unauthorized access to systems, data, or accounts.
  • Sharing confidential business or customer data with unauthorized parties.
  • Engaging in malicious activities such as hacking, phishing, or installing unauthorized software.
  • Using company resources for personal gain, illegal activities, or inappropriate content.
  • Excessive personal use of company resources, including internet bandwidth and storage.
  • Attempting to bypass security controls, firewalls, or access restrictions.

Security Responsibilities

  • Report security incidents, data breaches, or suspicious activities immediately to IT security personnel.
  • Avoid using unsecured public networks for business operations without a VPN.
  • Regularly update and secure company-provided devices to prevent cyber threats.
  • Adhere to the company's data backup and encryption policies.
  • Lock workstations when leaving desks unattended to prevent unauthorized access.

Monitoring and Auditing

  • The company reserves the right to monitor network activity, email usage, and access logs to ensure compliance with this policy.
  • Regular audits will be conducted to identify potential security risks and enforce best practices.
  • Users should have no expectation of privacy when using company resources.

Enforcement & Consequences

Violations of this AUP may result in disciplinary actions, including loss of access privileges, termination of employment, or legal action where necessary. Severe violations, such as data breaches or cybersecurity threats, may lead to criminal prosecution.
